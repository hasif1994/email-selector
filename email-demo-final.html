Developer Spec — Email Routing Logic demo only

When a user selects contacts from multiple companies, the system automatically splits recipients into To and Cc fields. One email is sent per company — not one blast to everyone.

To Primary Recipient

One contact per company goes in To. This is the highest-priority contact type selected for that company. If only one contact is selected, it always goes in To.

Cc Secondary Recipients

All remaining selected contacts from the same company go in Cc. They receive the same email but are treated as secondary recipients.

To-field priority order (highest → lowest)
Primary › Business › Contact 1 › Contact 2 › Contact 3

If a Primary contact is selected, they always get the To field regardless of what else is selected. If Primary is not selected but Business is, Business takes To. And so on down the chain.

Example — Globex Corp: Business + Contact 1 + Contact 2 selected
To homer@globex.com (Business — highest priority of selection)
Cc abrooks@globex.com, dscully@globex.com (Contact 1, Contact 2)
